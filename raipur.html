RAIPUR

Overview

Raipur, which was once a part of Madhya Pradesh, is the capital of the Indian state of Chhattisgarh. It is also known as the rice bowl of India because of the varieties of rice grown in the region. The Mahanadi river flows through the city.

It is one of the fastest growing cities of the country in terms of industries like coal, aluminium, power, steel and tourism. Doodhadhari Monastery, Vivekananda Sarovar, Vivekananda Ashram, Mahant Ghasidas Museum, Rajiv Gandhi Van and Urja Bhawan are some of the places to visit. One more important point of interest is the Nagar Ghadi which is a clock that plays the folk songs of Chhattisgarh. The weather is moderate throughout the year but the winter season is the best time to visit this place.


POPULAR PLACES

GHATARANI WATERFALLS

Ghatarani Waterfalls make for a great picnic spot in Raipur. Perched amidst dense vegetation, the moderate cascade can be reached after a short trek through the forest. The hike up to the waterfall is amazing, and one can feast their eyes on the wide-ranging flora and fauna inhabiting in the forest. This tiered waterfall is surrounded by lush greenery as the water gushes down the stone precipice forming milky white foam, making for an ethereal scenic beauty. The waterfall plunges down to create a small and calm pool which is idyllic for enjoying a refreshing sip during the scorching summer heat.

JATMAI TEMPLE

Set amidst a breathtaking lush green forest and beautified by the gushing Ghatarani waterfall, Jatmai Temple is one of the ancient places of worship in Raipur. The magnificently built temple is dedicated to Mata Jatmai and is delicately carved out of granite. Adorned with numerous towers, the main entrance of this temple carries murals that depict mythological characters. The shrine houses a stone idol that is placed inside the sanctum sanctorum. The temple wears a heavily festooned appearance during the occasion of Navaratri which is celebrated with much devotion and enthusiasm.

MAHANT GHASIDAS MUSEUM

Mahant Ghasidas Memorial Museum or Raipur Museum is Raipur’s prized possession. Set up by the Rajnandangaon Ruler, Raja Mahant Ghasidas, the museum cradles an astounding collection of ancient artifacts in the form of sculptures, pottery and exquisite artworks made of metal and stone. Shortly after the construction of this museum, Lord Cunningham from the Indian Archeological Service team found the remnant of Sirpur in Chhattisgarh which was instantly domiciled here as part of the institution’s valuable exhibits. Located in an idyllic and serene environment, the museum is surrounded by beautiful gardens and lawns beautified by life-size sculptures.

URJA PARK

When in Raipur, Urja Park is the best bet for your usual weekend outings. The park is situated at the Mana Road, in the heart of Raipur city and gets its name from the late Indian Prime Minister, Rajiv Gandhi. The uniqueness of this theme-based park is that it is entirely run on solar energy. With large green spaces, expertly maintained gardens, sparkling lake, a fine assortment of flora and fauna and other manmade marvels, this park is an idyllic spot for those seeking quiet refuge in an urban setting.

Languages Spoken

Hindi is spoken and understood by most of the population. Chhattisgarhi is also widely spoken here by the locals. Dialects like Gondi, Bhunjia and Halbi are spoken by the tribal people, namely the Bhunjia Adivasis. English is also spoken and understood in the urban regions.

Best Season to Visit

The best time to visit Raipur, India indisputably is October to March. The best time to travel to Chhattisgarh is imputed to the months spanning from October to March. Chhattisgarh has a tropical climate due to which mercury literally rises to as high as 45°C in the months of mid-April to June end.

Must try

1.Witness the Pola festival: Bullock Race. . This unique festival is entertained with the bull race that is extremely popular in this region and basically the main attraction too. It is an affair of the festival as it is followed by Pola and Teeja as teeja being the festival of women.
2.Marvel the Pandavani performance.Pandavani literally means stories or songs of the Pandavas {the brothers in the legend Mahabharata}. Pandavani act involves singing and enacting the narration of tales from the ancient Indian epic stories specifically ‘The Mahabharata’. Some famous dance acts performed are Raut Nacha, Dewar Nacha, Panthi and Soowa, Padki and Pandwani.

How to reach


BY FLIGHT

The domestic airport at Raipur is linked to all major airports in the country. Indian Airlines, Kingfisher red, Kingfisher Airlines and Jet Airways operate regular flights to /fro Chhattisgarh India.

BY RAIL

It has its own Railway Station named as Raipur Railway Station which is connected to the major cities of Chhattisgarh. It is well connected with the cities such as New Delhi, Mumbai, Bhopal, Jabalpur, Chennai, Bangalore, Kota, Ahmedabad and Jaipur via Bilaspur Express, Bilaspur Rajdhani, Howrah Sup Express, Pune Hatia Super fast and Pa Bilaspur Express.

BY ROAD

Raipur is 13 Kms from Barauda, 40 Kms from Durg, 76 Kms from Patewa, 79 Kms from Bhatapara, 113 Kms from Bilaspur, 284 Kms from Jagdalpur, 301 Kms from Dhanpunji and is linked through Chhattisgarh State Road Transport Corporation (CSRTC) and some private travel services.
