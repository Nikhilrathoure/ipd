JAISALMER

Overview

Dotted enchanting palaces, and temples, Jaisalmer is aptly named as ‘The Golden City.' The golden yellow sandstone used to build this fort led to this name. Located in the majestic state of Rajasthan, it is surrounded by the vast splendor of the mystical Thar Desert. This magical city is sure to invoke your inner curiosity. The breath-taking and massive Jaisalmer Fort makes you believe that you are in the midst of your favorite childhood fairy tale. Built in the 12th century, it is one of the largest fortifications and conveys its glorious history, having withstood numerous wars.

Exploring the desert via a camel safari is a unique experience altogether. The powerful winds create beautiful patterns and textures on the sand dunes. Jaisalmer is blessed with a vibrant culture. The markets of Jaisalmer are brimming with shops selling exquisite handicrafts, which are unique to the city. If you wish to try traditional Rajasthani cuisine, visit any of the several restaurants located in the city. You can also get a peek inside the local culture by interacting with the locals and getting to know their lifestyle.


POPULAR PLACES

JAISALMER FORT

One of the largest forts in Rajasthan, Jaisalmer Fort, is a must-see attraction in Jaisalmer. It was built in 1156 A.D by Raja Rawal Jaiswal on the hilltop of Trikuta. Also known as Sonar Quila or the Golden Fort, it captivates you with its tawny lion colour of yellow sandstones during the day which is followed by the honey-gold hues in the evening that also camouflages the fort in the yellow desert. Located in the very heart of the city with around 3000 people residing in its walls, Jaisalmer Fort is a fine example of an urban living centre.

AMAR SAGAR LAKE

On the outskirts of Western Jaisalmer, approximately 4 Km from Bada Bagh, lies Amar Sagar Lake. Beautiful and serene, a series of steps from the Amar Singh Palace leads you to the lake. The complex of the palace and lake comprise numerous wells and ponds along with an ancient Shiva temple. According to some local tales, these wells were built by the prostitutues.

PATWAON KI HAVELI

Constructed by brothers of Jain religion, the Patwon Ki Haveliyan is an architectural marvel of the 19th century. It is a cluster of five distinct palaces that are one of the most prominent attractions in Jaisalmer. Famous for their exclusive mirror work and paintings, you get to see the creative prowess of the ancient craftsmen here. The first haveli has been converted into a museum and is the biggest of them all. It includes a vast reserve of antique furniture and is the most ostentatious.

SAM SAND DUNES

One of the most popular excursions, 41 Km west of Jaisalmer, is Sam Sand Dunes. Your trip to Jaisalmer would be incomplete if you would think of skipping this exciting spot. It gives you the best sunrise and sunset views that the Thar Desert can offer.

Languages Spoken

Rajasthani is the most widely spoken language in Jaisalmer. Hindi, which is the official language of the state, is also spoken widely. Urdu is also spoken by some people. People working in the tourism industry are fluent in English as well.

Best Season to Visit

The best time to visit Jaisalmer is during the winter season, between the months of October and March. The weather is very enjoyable during this time, and you can indulge in various adventurous activities in the desert as well as sightseeing.

Must try

1.The Gadisar Lake is among the most popular Jaisalmer attractions. Boating here is one of the refreshing things to do in Jaisalmer.
2.Parasailing-Kanoi.This thrilling and brave heart parasailing activity is quite common in the desert areas and one of the mesmerizing Jaisalmer attractions.
3.Paramotoring is among the must-try adventure activities in Jaisalmer for those who wish to make the Jaisalmer trip daring and adventurous.
4.Visiting the Tanot Mata Temple is one of the most popular things to do at Jaisalmer Indo-Pak border. Located near the Tanot Mata Temple, the Indo-Pak border is among the most sought-after tourist attractions in Jaisalmer.

Shopping

Unlike shopping malls, high-end boutiques, and designer labels that we find in metropolitan cities, shopping in Jaisalmer offers a completely unique experience to all the travelers alike.Manas Chowk-Manas Chowk may be small in size, there is no dearth of shapes, colors, and variety of the souvenirs that you can buy here.Pansari Bazaar-considered to be one of the oldest street markets in the city, it is also referred to as the Villager’s market which is famous for selling items which are ethnic to the core. The look and feel of shopping in Jaisalmer can be best experience at the very own Sadar Bazaar. Although the market it famous for its leather goods, one can literally get their hands on anything from paintings, carpets, and handicrafts to jewelry, shoes, and sarees.

How to reach


BY FLIGHT

Jaisalmer does not have an airport of its own. Tourists travelling by air can reach Jodhpur Airport, which is 300 km away, and take a bus, taxi or train to Jaisalmer. The airport is maintained by the Indian Air Force and has a civil enclave. It is a domestic airport and has regular flights to Bangalore, Mumbai and Delhi via Jet Airways and Air India.

BY RAIL

Jaisalmer Railway Station is administered by North Western Railway of the Indian Railways. It is connected to several towns and cities in India such as Jodhpur, Kolkata, Mumbai and Delhi. Some of the trains operating from here are Ranikhet Express, Jaisalmer - Jodhpur Weekly Special, Jaisalmer - Howrah SF Express and Jaisalmer - Bandra Terminus SF Express. A luxury train named ‘Palace on Wheels’ also passes through Jaisalmer.

BY ROAD

National Highway No. 15 passes through Jaisalmer. It is well-connected by road to various cities and towns in India. Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation (RSRTC) is a public sector transport corporation serving Rajasthan. It has a fleet of buses and has various depots within the state as well as outside the state. Many private buses also ply from cities in Rajasthan and its neighbouring states such as Jaipur, Jodhpur, Ahmedabad, Mumbai and Pune. These buses include A/C and non-A/C buses, deluxe and semi-deluxe buses as well as ordinary buses. Pooja Travels, Kalpana Travels and Geetanjali Travels are some of the private bus operators.
